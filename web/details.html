Functionality Arguiew features and inner working

About Functionality

nada

Review parsing

From a URL pointing to a list of reviews, each review is encapsulated into an object. Text, features, and other relevant data are stored. Arguiew manipulates these objects in order to find hidden relations among them. MORE HERE.

Features extraction

Text is rendered into machine-readable features (or keywords or categories, as you prefer). We take care of putting everything into the right bucket, e.g. "small", "tiny" and "huge" fall into the "size" feature; we also try to detect implicit references, e.g. "good picture quality" falls into the "camera sensor" feature. Furthermore, we try to capture multiple features out of a single one, e.g. a positive reference to "nightlife" implies a negative reference to "quietness".

Debate generation

We read reviews to come up with a general idea about which product features are good, and then decide upon how these match our expectations. A single review represents an opinion. When adding more reviews, conflict arises; it's up to us to come up with a conclusion! If there were no conflict, this is straightforward, but we know this is not generally the case. Arguiew organises reviews as being part of a (virtual) debate. Compatible reviews are teamed up and redundant reviews are inserted into the most general review in order to yield a compact graphical representation. The user can thus decide upon this drawing instead of going through hundreds of reviews.

Automated reasoning

Arguiew offers three alternatives for obtaining the consensus: (1) "Feature counting" checks positive and negative occurrences for every feature. If there are more positive than negative, the conclusion is that the consensus about that feature is a positive opinion. (2) "Feature rating" assigns a rating to each review according to a measure of compatibility with regard to the rest of the reviews. Features inherit different ratings according to the reviews they are in; the total rating (the sum) of positive opinions for each feature is checked against the total rating of negative opinions for that feature, and a conclusion is drawn. (3) "Argumentation" organises reviews as a debate held by reviewers. Pairs of conflicting reviews are resolved by giving preference to the higher-rating one. Cycles are resolved to get rid of irrelevant sub-debates and the final network is evaluated following computational argumentation techniques. The result is a consistent subset of reviews whose features are to be believed in.

Visualisation examples

Bring to your mind an imaginary hotel and rate it according to your own experience. Be objective (and creative if you must). Your review will be added to the list of reviews below. Note that these reviews are already put in a graph. Once you enter yours and hit the "Evaluate" button, you'll see how the graph changes to accommodate a depiction of your review, including the features you mentioned.

Positive and Negative Opinions
Important:

The idea of writing reviews is to provide an informed yet brief opinion. Try to be consistent, clear and objective.

Arguments Graph

Each node in the graph below corresponds to a review. Edges between reviews represent conflict. Nodes are labelled with their features and edges, with the conflicting features between the pair of nodes they relate. A compression tool keeps the graph small when the number of reviews grow.

General Stats

Apart from the graphic visualisation, we also provide general statistics about the features, such as: positive and negative frequency, compatibility rating, and so on. Decisions could be drawn from these numbers.

Language Domain Lanaguges, Domains, Scopes...

About Language Domains

We will identify different domains where our tools applies well. Also different human languages are supported.

Domains

Nuestra herramienta se adapata a cualquier dominio, en el cual los reviews sean homogeneos sobre ese dominio. Es importante entender la necesidad de conocer previamente comentarios del dominio particular para poder entrenar los algoritmos especificos.

Language

Nuestra herramienta puede evolucionar para entener distintos lenguages humanos, incluso dialectos. Esta es una tarea critica, pero el entramiento con comentarios conocidos en un determinado lenguaje, hace que los algoritmos puedan aprender relaciones entre palabras de un lenguaje desconocido y el impacto en las opniones.
